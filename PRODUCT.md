# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Small and mid-size business owners deciding whether to hire Ryo for their own business's website — a restaurant, studio, boutique, or similar operation sizing up whether this level of craft is within reach for them. They arrive with some skepticism about whether a freelancer can deliver agency-grade work, and are comparing what they'd get here against both template-site pricing and full agency pricing. Site 26 (The Tideline, a restaurant) is the clearest embodiment of this audience: a real business type a visitor should be able to picture themselves in.

## Product Purpose

The Blueprint Labs exists to win freelance client work by proving code-driven design capability across a wide range of business verticals and aesthetic styles. Every one of the 26 sites is real, running code — not a mockup, comp, or template reskin — spanning genuinely different creative and technical directions so a visitor can find something that maps to their own kind of business. Success is a direct inquiry through the Hub's contact channels (email, Fastwork, and LINE — LINE matters here given the target market).

## Positioning

A portfolio where every demonstration is real, working code across deliberately varied creative and technical directions, priced and presented so a visitor concludes this is more polished than what they'd normally expect for the price — proof that skilled, code-driven design is reachable at a freelance budget, not gated behind agency rates.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: direct contact — email, Fastwork profile, and LINE (placeholder values, marked `TODO:` in the Hub footer until real values are supplied).

## Brand Personality

Precise, ambitious, confident. The "blueprint" name carries engineering-grade precision; the 26 sites carry the ambition. Confidence shows up as restraint at the Hub level — per DESIGN_NOTES.md, the Hub frame stays quiet (tinted near-black, warm paper text, one accent) so the loud, varied showcase sites do the talking, not the frame around them.

## Anti-references

Should never read as: a generic template portfolio (Wix/Squarespace-shaped hero + grid-of-cards with no real interactivity); a corporate agency site leaning on stock handshake photography and empty "digital excellence" jargon; or an AI-slop tech demo that looks impressive in isolation but doesn't read as something a paying client could picture for their own business. The reservations flow on site 26 is the model for the difference — a real product interaction, not a showcase gimmick.

## Design Principles

- Every demo must be real, running code a visitor can click through — no mockups, no static comps standing in for interaction.
- Range over repetition: no repeated vertical+style combination across the 25 sites: prove breadth, not one trick reskinned.
- The Hub frame stays quiet so the work stays loud — restraint at the container level, ambition inside each site.
- Honest positioning: no fabricated experience, testimonials, or client history, on the Hub or anywhere in the project.
- Relatability over exclusivity: a visitor should recognize the *tier* of business they'd actually hire this for (see site 26's $$$-not-ultra-luxury calibration), not feel priced out.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline only, per CLAUDE.md: alt text on all imagery, visible focus states, semantic HTML, and full `prefers-reduced-motion` support with a static (not degraded) fallback everywhere animation is used. No formal WCAG level target or additional accommodation requirements beyond this baseline.
