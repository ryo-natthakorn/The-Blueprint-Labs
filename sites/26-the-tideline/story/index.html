Our story

The tide writes the menu.
We just keep up.

The Tideline is built around one idea: cook what the tide gives you. Our kitchen sources same-day from the harbor docks and three coastal farms within twenty miles — the menu changes with what showed up that morning, not what a printed card says it should be. No dish is permanent. That's the point.

There's no printed menu in the window and no laminated card on the table. There's a chalk wall, a tide clock behind the bar, and a kitchen that starts every day at the dock not knowing exactly what it will cook by five. Fourteen years in, we wouldn't run it any other way.

One day on the tideline

From first light to last table

4:45 AM

The dock

The day-boats tie up before first light, and whoever's cooking fish that night is standing on the quay when they do. What comes off the boats is the menu — there is no plan B, and after fourteen years the boats know it.

7:00 AM

The chalk wall

Back at the restaurant, the catch is broken down and the wall gets rewritten — starters on the left, mains on the right, yesterday's dishes gone for good. If the handwriting looks hurried, the fishing was good.

3:00 PM

The farm boxes

Saltmeadow, Ledgerow and Bright Hollow send their vans down the coast road after lunch. What's in the boxes decides the sides, the risotto, and the whole of the Tide Vegetable Plate. We don't send a list; they send what's ready.

5:00 PM

Doors

Family meal at 4:15, tide clock checked at 4:59 — old habit, the dining room looks straight down the channel and high water is worth seating for. Then the first tables come up the harbor road, and the day the tide wrote gets cooked.

Where it all comes from

Twenty miles, no further

Harrow's Point Town Dock 0.4 mi

Day-boat fish and shellfish, landed before dawn and walked up the quay. Whatever the raw bar holds tonight came off these boats this morning.

Saltmeadow Farm 11 mi

Brassicas, roots, tomatoes and the salt-tolerant greens that love this coast. Third-generation rows on the estuary flats.

Ledgerow Orchard 14 mi

Stone fruit, plums for the duck, and pressed juice for the zero-proof list. The charred plum on tonight's menu grew on a tree with a sea view.

Bright Hollow Dairy 19 mi

Cultured butter, crème fraîche and the stracciatella under the heirloom tomatoes. The furthest thing on the menu from the water, at nineteen miles.
