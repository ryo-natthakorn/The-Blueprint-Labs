The ocean is a building
six kilometres tall.

Bathyal operates crewed and autonomous descents through every floor of it. This page is one continuous dive — scroll, and mind the pressure.

−150 m · Epipelagic

The last of the weather. Baitfish shoal where the light still reaches; everything below here runs on what sinks.

−700 m · Mesopelagic

The twilight zone. Our acoustic arrays log the largest animal migration on Earth here, nightly, in the dark.

−1,600 m · Bathypelagic

No sunlight has ever reached this water. Four out of five animals at this depth make their own — move your pointer, and so will you.

−3,000 m · Abyssal plain approach

Pressure: three hundred atmospheres. Our vehicles are titanium spheres pretending to be indifferent.

−4,500 m · Something large

The cameras catch a silhouette perhaps once a season. We name them; they ignore us.

−6,000 m · Station VI

A hydrothermal vent field and the institute's deepest lab. Chemosynthesis, tube worms, water at 380 °C that cannot boil. This is the ground floor of the planet.
