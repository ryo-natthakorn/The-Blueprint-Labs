One hundred and forty-two components. Five years of drawing. Scroll, and the watch will come apart in your hands — it keeps running the whole way down. The hands you see hold the actual time.

I — Crystal

Sapphire, domed
by flame.

Grown as a single crystal boule, sliced, then ground for eleven hours against diamond paste. It will scratch only if the world ends. Double anti-reflective coating makes it disappear entirely.

II — Dial & hands

Champagne, brushed
in one direction.

Solid silver, sunray-brushed from the pinion outward. The hands are flame-blued steel — heated to exactly 290°C, the moment the oxide layer turns cornflower. One degree more and you start again.

III — Gear train

Four wheels,
one argument with physics.

The barrel turns once every six hours; the escape wheel, ten times a second. Between them, four gold wheels negotiate a 36,000-fold gearing in the space of a coin.

IV — Mainspring & rotor

A steel ribbon
holding its breath.

Sixty-five centimeters of alloy coiled into a barrel twelve millimeters wide. The tungsten rotor rewinds it with every movement of your wrist — ninety hours of reserve, wound by walking.

V — Caseback

Signed where
only you will look.

Every calibre is engraved with its assembler's initials and regulated in six positions over fifteen days. Drag the exploded stack to turn it. Scroll back up to reassemble.
