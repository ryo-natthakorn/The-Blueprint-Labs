GRAMMAGE fine paper goods — 90 gsm and upward

Begin with a sheet
that deserves it.

Cotton rag, 90 grams to the square metre. Scroll, and fold along with us.

↓

I. The spine

Halve it and open it again. The first crease is the only one the paper will remember forever — make it a promise, not a guess.

II. The shoulders

Both corners bow to the centreline. Good paper resists just enough to tell you where the fibre runs.

III. The taper

Again, steeper. The sheet stops being a page here and starts being an intention.

IV. The keel

Close it on the first promise. Everything the paper has learned is now stacked seven layers deep.

V. The wings

One fold each side, parallel to the keel. Grammage is aerodynamics, if you're honest about it.

VI. Flight test

Every sheet we sell has survived this exact ritual at least once, somewhere in the studio, after hours.
